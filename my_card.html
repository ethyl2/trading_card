Baker's Yeast

Yeast love eating sugar in a warm, moist environment. They are very helpful in making bread and other foods.

  • Scientific Name: Saccharomyces cerevisiae
  • Average Length: 5–10 μm in diameter
  • Average Lifespan: Depends on environmental conditions and temperature
  • Habitat: They are found in the wild growing on grapes and other fruits. They can be cultivated in liquid media or on a nutrient surface.

Yeast has been used since ancient Egyptians first figured out how to use them to make wine and bread. In making bread, the yeast eat sugar, and carbon dioxide is a byproduct (in a process called fermentation), which creates holes in the bread. Yeast divide by budding -- a small bud emerges from the surface of the parent and enlarges until it is almost the same size. They are also helpful in scientific research. In fact, Saccharomyces cerevisiae was the first organism to have its genome (its DNA sequence) fully sequenced.
